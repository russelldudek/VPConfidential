First 120 days

Earn the right to reshape the network.

The sequence begins with listening and evidence, not a preloaded reorganization.

Days 0-30

Read the promise

Map customer commitments, safety and quality risk, critical technologies, current capacity logic, leadership topology, active expansions, and decision bottlenecks.

Days 31-60

Make the shape visible

Build the first enterprise capability-capacity map, reconcile metrics, define decision rights, and identify the few constraints that dominate customer and margin exposure.

Days 61-90

Prove the contract

Run one cross-site capacity or NPI decision through the new evidence standard, establish governance cadence, and validate the operating scorecard with site leaders.

Days 91-120

Commit the portfolio

Present a sequenced manufacturing strategy: protect, improve, flex, expand, partner, or stop - with capital logic, owners, risks, and leadership capability actions.

The hardest reasonable question

Can Russell translate adjacent scale into credible global manufacturing leadership?

The affirmative case is not title equivalence. It is the combination of advanced electronics manufacturing, high-reliability quality systems, 24/7 distributed operations, robotics and machine-vision operations, technical materials grounding, and direct operating-mechanism construction.

The first-120-day approach is designed to make transferability observable quickly: listen before prescribing, expose the current decision system, prove one consequential cross-site mechanism, then scale only what earns trust.

Executive interview questions

Questions that surface the real mandate.

  1. 01Where does the current manufacturing network most often break the customer promise: capacity, quality, technology readiness, decision latency, or leadership depth?
  2. 02Which manufacturing differences are strategic capabilities that must be protected, and which are merely historical variation?
  3. 03What capital or footprint decision will this leader need to make in the first year, and what evidence is currently missing?
  4. 04How are NPI readiness and technology transfer governed today across business, engineering, quality, supply chain, and sites?
  5. 05What would the CEO and Global Supply Chain leader say must be measurably different twelve months after this hire?
