## Company

World-class supplier of materials and process solutions for high-tech industries. This position does not require relocation.

## Position

The Vice President, Global Manufacturing Strategy will serve as a key member of the Global Supply Chain leadership team. This executive will lead the company's global manufacturing organization and develop strategies that enable operational excellence, scalable manufacturing capacity, and strong execution as the organization enters its next phase of growth. The VP will oversee a complex global manufacturing network and support growth objectives, customer commitments, profitability goals, and operational excellence initiatives.

The successful candidate will bring deep experience operating within highly technical manufacturing environments and balance near-term execution with long-term manufacturing strategy. This leader will champion safety, quality, accountability, and continuous improvement across a geographically diverse footprint while ensuring readiness for future growth.

## Primary responsibilities

- Lead global manufacturing with accountability for safety, quality, delivery, cost, productivity, customer satisfaction.
- Develop and execute global manufacturing strategy aligned with growth, customer demand, operating requirements, and long-term plans.
- Lead capacity planning, footprint optimization, capital strategy, and make-versus-buy decisions.
- Ensure manufacturing readiness for NPI, technology transitions, and evolving customer requirements.
- Drive advanced manufacturing, automation, digital technologies, and continuous improvement.
- Standardize systems, metrics, governance, and best practices across the network.
- Improve cost competitiveness, asset utilization, productivity, margin, quality, reliability, and service.
- Build and retain a world-class leadership team, succession, and talent capability.
- Partner across business and functions for growth, integration, and expansion.

## Requirements

Bachelor's degree in engineering, business, or related field; MBA preferred. At least 15 years of progressive manufacturing leadership, including at least five years in a senior executive role leading global multi-site operations. Experience in advanced materials, specialty chemicals, industrial technology, electronics, precision manufacturing, or related highly engineered process environments. Demonstrated transformations, expansions, productivity and margin improvements, capital and cost management, talent development, global matrix leadership, data-driven change, and up to 50% travel.
