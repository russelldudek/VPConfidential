First 120 days

Earn the right to reshape the network.

An evidence-first entry plan designed to build trust, expose the operating system, and prove one consequential mechanism before scaling change.

Guiding principles

Listen before standardizing

Understand the technical and historical reason for variation before classifying it as waste.

Protect the customer promise

Do not trade safety, quality, delivery, or recovery credibility for transformation theater.

Baseline before targets

Reconcile definitions, units, ownership, and data confidence before setting commitments.

Prove before scaling

Use one live decision to test governance, evidence, and behavior.

Days 91-120 - Commit the manufacturing portfolio
Manufacturing strategy deliverable
Protect

Strategic process capability, customer-critical capacity, safety and quality systems, technical centers, leadership depth.

Why it matters
Improve

Yield, uptime, labor productivity, flow, maintenance, quality loss, working capital and decision latency.

Evidence first
Flex / Partner

Qualified alternate capacity, make-buy boundaries, surge response, supplier and contract manufacturing posture.

Optionality
Expand / Stop

Sequenced capital, footprint changes, technology moves and sunset decisions with explicit owners and review dates.

Portfolio choice
Leadership outputs

Operating calendar

Weekly, monthly, quarterly and annual review architecture tied to real decisions.

Leadership bench map

Critical roles, successors, capability gaps, development moves and retention risk.

Capital portfolio

Prioritized investments, assumptions, alternatives, timing, risk and benefits.

Transformation backlog

Bounded work by customer risk, economics, technical readiness and change burden.

Measures of a credible first 120 days
  • Leaders can describe the same manufacturing priorities and decision rights.
  • The top customer, capacity, technical and leadership risks are visible with owners.
  • One consequential cross-site decision has been improved by the new evidence standard.
  • The scorecard uses agreed definitions and exposes confidence rather than hiding it.
  • The portfolio distinguishes what to protect, improve, flex, expand, partner or stop.
